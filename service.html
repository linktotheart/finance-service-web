NBFC 101

Non- NBFC is registered under Companies Act,1956 indulged in the business of providing loans and advances, acquisition of shares, stocks etc. issued by the Government or local authority or other marketable securities of like nature, leasing, hire-purchase, chit business and insurance business but not the businesses whose principal business is that of agriculture activities, industrial activities, purchase or sale of any goods or providing any services and sale, purchase or construction of immovable property. NBFC are different from banks and are regulated by the rules of Reserve Bank of India. Chapter IIIB of the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 provides the framework for NBFC. There are certain criteria which is requisite for any NBFC to comply including registering under company’s act and a minimum net owned funds of Rs.2 crore. To determine the company is into financial business or not RBI (Reserve Bank of India) apply a test to the institution, popularly known as 50-50 test. Purpose of RBI is to examine whether principal business of the institute is financial activity or not. Financial activity as principal business is a situation where a company’s financial assets constitute more than 50% of the total assets and income from financial assets constitute more than 50% of the gross income. A company to be registered as NBFC by RBI must clear this test.

Activities of NBFC are akin to that of banks but they are not banks. Their activities seems to be same but there are many differences between a bank and a NBFC.
Any NBFC must not be confused with bank and to clarify this dilemma, differences are mentioned here.

  • NBFC cannot issue self-drawn cheques and demand drafts, unlike banks.
  • NBFC do not accept demand deposit but banks do accept demand deposits.
  • Banks form an integral part of payment and settlement system, but NBFCs does not.
  • It is mandatory for banks to maintain Reserve ratios but it is not the case with NBFC.
  • Deposit insurance facility of Deposit Insurance and Credit Guarantee Corporation is available to the customers of banks but NBFC does not provide such services.
  • Foreign investments are allowed up to 100% to the NBFC unlike banks.

NBFC are regulated under Rules of Reserve Bank of India and it duty of the government to vigilate the establishment and working of any NBFC. Certain permissions and certificates are requisite which are authorised by the government authorities after scrutiny of the documents and attachments required by the authorities.

  • To commence the business of NBFC, a company registered under Companies act, with the object clause of financial activity must company must obtain a certificate of Registration (CoR) from Reserve Bank of India.
  • An application with required documents shall be submitted in the prescribed form with the Reserve Bank of India. After scrutiny of the application and the attachments, Reserve Bank of India being satisfied that its provisions are complies with, would issue Certificate of Registration to the applicant.
  • Application (COSMOS) and all mandatory documents can be filed online on the website of RBI. But it is advised that a company should try to register as an NBFC after satisfaction of the some essentials, that include
    • that the company shall be registered under the Companies Act;
    • a minimum Net Owned Funds (NOF) of INR 2 crores to set up a company in India;
    • one of the directors shall be experienced in the similar field of business or shall be an experienced banker
    • CIBIL records of the company, shareholders and director must be free from any regularities.
  • Physical copy of application and all its attachment shall be submitted to the RBI after obtaining application reference number (ARN) from Reserve Bank of India. Using this ARN, an applicant can track its application. RBI issues this ARN only after successful submission of the application.
  • License of registration of NBFC is then granted by RBI after receiving application and scrutiny of company profile, promoter/director and shareholder profile.
